Divvy Case Study

Objective

Convert the casual client basis to annual pass membership holders.

Insights

  • Casual riders are more likely to use Divvy services on weekends rather than weekdays.
  • Casual riders prefer elecric bikes (59% of trips) to the classic bikes (41% of trips).
  • Casual riders rent Divvy bikes longer (22 minute average) vs. annual membership holders (12 minute average).

Solutions

  • Implement a new annual membership tier for weekend only usage at a lower price point.
  • Offer an introductory promotion to casual users for their first classic bike rental experience.
  • Expand electric bike holding locations and stock.

Meet Divvy, a ride-share company owned by the Chicago Department of Transit (CDOT) and operated in partnership with Lyft. Divvy serves the cities of Chicago and Evanstan with recent expansions in the Far South Side of Chicago in 2020 as well as both Northwest and Southwest Chicago in 2021. More recently, Divvy redesigned their ebike and through partnership with Skyhook Solar, in 2022, they launched solar powered ebike canopy docking stations. Their mission is to provide a convenient alternative for consumers to travel Chicago. To do this, they have both pedal (classical) and electrical bikes (ebikes) ready on demand for rent to consumers. data, methodology, and tools

This case study uses 12-months internal, first-party collected data between February 2022 - January 2023 provided by Divvy under this license. Each row of data is comprised of anonymized metadata under the domains of: time, location, and type. The data given is presented in the form of .csv spreadsheets broken up by month of occurance. The methodology guiding this case study follows the teachings of the Google Data Analytics Professional Certification, which is: Ask, Prepare, Process, Analyze, Share, and Act. For this study, I utilized the programming languages: Python, SQL, and VBA.

Ask

  • Identify historical trends for casual bike riders
  • Determine factors that influence casual riders into buying annual memberships
  • Use insights from historical trends and factors associated with casual riders buying annual memberships to improve the casual rider to annual membership conversion rate via digital media.
